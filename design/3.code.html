Samadhi Pada 1.2 arrow_forward

yogaś citta-vṛtti-nirodhaḥ

योगश्चित्तवृत्तिनिरोधः

"Yoga is the restriction of the fluctuations of consciousness (citta)."

— Primary Translation
Samadhi Pada 1.30 arrow_forward

vyādhi-styāna-saṃśaya-pramāda-ālasya-avirati-bhrāntidarśana-alabdhabhūmikatva-anavasthitatvāni citta-vikṣepās te ’ntarāyāḥ

व्याधिस्त्यानसंशयप्रमादालस्याविरतिभ्रान्तिदर्शनालब्धभूमिकत्वानवस्थितत्वानि चित्तविक्षेपास्तेऽन्तरायाः

"Sickness, apathy, doubt, carelessness, laziness, hedonism, delusion, lack of progress, and instability are the distractions of the mind (citta) which cause the obstacles."

— Primary Translation
Samadhi Pada 1.33 arrow_forward

maitrī-karuṇā-mudito-pekṣāṇāṁ-sukha-duḥkha-puṇyā-puṇya-viṣayāṇāṁ bhāvanātaś citta-prasādanam

मैत्रीकरुणामुदितोपेक्षाणां सुखदुःखपुण्यापुण्यविषयाणां भावनातश्चित्तप्रसादनम्

"By cultivating attitudes of friendliness toward the happy, compassion for the unhappy, delight in the virtuous, and disregard toward the wicked, the mind-stuff (citta) retains its undisturbed calmness."

— Primary Translation
Samadhi Pada 1.37 arrow_forward

vītarāga-viṣayaṁ vā cittam

वीतरागविषयं वा चित्तम्

"Or that mind (citta) which has given up all attachment to sense objects."

— Primary Translation
