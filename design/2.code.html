योगश्चित्तवृत्तिनिरोधः

yogaś-citta-vṛtti-nirodhaḥ

yogaḥ = yoga • citta = consciousness • vṛtti = fluctuations • nirodhaḥ = restraint
BKS

B.K.S. Iyengar

Light on the Yoga Sutras

"Yoga is the cessation of movements in the consciousness."
Iyengar emphasizes that *citta* (consciousness) includes mind, intelligence, and ego. The restraint of these fluctuations leads to the experience of the soul.
SS

Swami Satchidananda

The Yoga Sutras

"The restraint of the modifications of the mind-stuff is Yoga."
Satchidananda uses the term "mind-stuff" to describe *citta*. He compares the mind to a lake; when the surface is calm, you can see the bottom clearly.
EB

Edwin Bryant

North Point Press

"Yoga is the stilling of the changing states of the mind."
Bryant offers a more academic perspective, translating *nirodhah* as "stilling" rather than complete suppression, emphasizing focus.
